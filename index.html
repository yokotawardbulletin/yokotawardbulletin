Sacrament Meeting Theme: Diligence

"I usually place a quote here, with a link to the talk or video it came from, but this week, I wanted to share my testimony and my gratitude. I am grateful for my calling, where I get to design the ward bulletin and share it with all of you, and I'm grateful for it for a few reasons. I get to stay connected with what is happening in the ward. I always know about upcoming events. I am stretched technically as I try to find ways to improve with limited training and resources. I get to be of service. And, I get to search, really really search, for the right scripture or talk to share, which always helps me to pause and listen to the spirit. In whatever calling we have, however big or small and however often it is or isn't noticed by others, we receive a chance to work with the savior on behalf of others and we are asked to pause and listen to the spirit guide us. I wanted to share my testimony at the start of this new year, that just as the Savior is the head of this church, He can steer our lives through any storm, and though He may not always calm the tumultuous seas in our lives, He will lift us up to walk safely on the water of our afflictions we struggled hopelessly against on our own. I hope that anyone reading this believes me when I say I know my Redeemer lives, and He is there for all of us this year and every year to come, as He has been for all mankind every year past, and the enabling power of His living Atonement can strengthen us far beyond our imaginings and beyond the reach of every adversity."

- Harrison Merrill
