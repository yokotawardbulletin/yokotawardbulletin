Spiritual Thought - 2021 First Presidency Christmas Message

"When we sing 'Silent Night,' we know the life of that Babe of Bethlehem did not begin there, nor did it end on Calvary. In a premortal realm, Jesis was foreordained to atone for us. He was wounded for out transgressions and bruised for our iniquities. He came to make immortality a reality and eternal life a possibility for all who would ever live.
At this Sacred Christmas season, we testify that our loving Heavenly Father 'so loved the world, that he gave his only begotten Son, that whosoever believeth in him should not perish, but have everlasting life.'"

- The First Presidency of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints - President Russell M. Nelson, President Dallin H. Oaks and President Henry B. Eyring, 2021 First Presidency Christmas Message, © 2021 by Intellectual Reserve Inc.

Message from the Tokyo South Stake Presidency

New!  "As we prepare to celebrate the birth of our Savior, Jesus Christ, we have the great opportunity to participate in the Church's 'Light the World' campaign. This year's theme is 'Light the World with Love.' Jesus Christ is the light of the world and we share His light as we love those around us through simple acts of kindness and service.
 Speaking of this initiative, Elder Marcus B. Nash of the Seventy said, 'We can hold up His light by doing what He did: to love God and love our neighbor by lifting up those who are down, helping fulfill the needs of those in want, and comforting those who hurt. Doing this will cause His light to shine in and through us and will bring light and peace to our troubled world one person at a time.''
 We invite everyone to join us in participating in this great initiative to find opportunities to love as the Savior loved. As you serve Him, you will also be blessed to find opportunities to share and invite others to 'Come and see' the message of peace and love that Christ offers all of us. We hope and pray you have a beautiful Christmas season as we all Light the World in Love."

- Message Written by Brother Brad Brough, High Councilor over Missionary Work

Bishop Larson's Challenge

Starting from today, read 7 days without missing a day and I will give the primary kids a piece of candy on Sunday every week they complete the challenge. If they can’t have candy I can also do a small 3D printed prize. I’m trying to help the primary kids form a habit of daily personal scripture reading. Also 1 verse is the minimum, if they are already reading more than that then don’t slow down or read less, just read AT LEAST 1 verse everyday no matter what. If you miss a day then start again the next day but try to keep your streak alive.

Bishop Larson also challenges youth and adults to read at least one chapter a day in the scriptures.
