Message from the Bishop

February - So we are one month down and eleven more to go in 2021. Looking at our Ward Mission plan we finished with a solid 151 invitations, which is a great start! And I want to take a moment to clear up any confusion as to what ‚Äòcounts‚Äô as an invitation. If you extend an invitation to do something ‚Äî anything ‚Äî and then you are wondering if that counted or not...it counts! Please don‚Äôt stress over it. The theme of the plan is ‚ÄúSmall and Simple‚Äù so any small and simple invitation counts.

Let me give a few examples of things I have seen in the ward. I have seen people reach out to someone they don‚Äôt hang out with everyday and invite them to lunch (3 invitations for the 3 people that were invited). Reaching out to someone that you think might need a little help and watch their child for a few hours so they can have some peace or get stuff done‚Äîcount it. Inviting someone new over for some extra dessert you made‚Äîcount those people. Remember, the Lord works through us to bless the lives of others. Last Saturday night we had the thought to invite someone over for dinner and we got like 3 invitations out of it because people had already been invited somewhere else! (Props to all of you! And pro tip... wait until Saturday night to invite over for Sunday dinner üòâ ). Does inviting members count? Yes. What about non-members? Yes. What about local nationals? Yes. Even stuffed animals? No, thats just weird.

Keep in mind this principle: the Lord always meets us where we are then lifts us up. Start small then work to bigger invitations. Let the spirit guide you as you look to show Christlike love by extending an invitation. Some people see the ward goal as very difficult. But remember, the Lord does not expect us to feed the 5000 ourselves, we just have to provide some bread and fish and He will do the rest. The second part of the plan should help to focus you on what you can do. This involves making a Family Mission Plan. Pray, seek the guidance of the spirit to know who to invite, what to invite to, where you can start to extend invitations to those things you are already doing and extend those invitations consistently and diligently. Jot those things down on a piece of paper and then put that paper somewhere where you will see it daily. Figure out what the Lord expects of you and how many invitations he wants you to contribute. Then go forth with faith and He will open those doors to help you reach your goals. I echo my original promise that as we diligently seek to reach our ward goal and extend those invitations as the spirit prompts we will start to see miracles happen in the ward. We have an amazing Ward family and I hope you feel the love we have for you.

- Bishop Nathan Larson

Yokota Emergency Preparedness Fair - Please join the Yokota Ward on 20 March at 10:00am for an in-person activity at the church building or virtually via zoom. This event is to assist families and individuals prepare for a possible emergency. Members are encouraged to participate and may invite friends, neighbors and co-workers. The fair will consist of a 15 minute introduction followed by smaller socially distanced groups that will move around the church to view, obtain ideas and ask questions on preparing a 72 hour "go kits". At the conclusion attendees will be able to enjoy breakfast refreshments served in the parking lot. Attendees are encouraged to use the West side bowling alley parking lot to alleviate limited parking at the church.
