Sacrament Meeting Theme: Being a Light to the World

"The world in which we live would benefit greatly if men and women everywhere would exercise the pure love of Christ, which is kind, meek, and lowly. It is without envy or pride. It is selfless because it seeks nothing in return. It does not countenance evil or ill will, nor rejoice in iniquity; it has no place for bigotry, hatred, or violence. It refuses to condone ridicule, vulgarity, abuse, or ostracism. It encourages diverse people to live together in Christian love regardless of religious belief, race, nationality, financial standing, education, or culture."

Walking the Savior's Path of Charity Teachings of the Presidents of the Church, Chapter 20:62, President Howard W. Hunter, First Presidency.

This quote was also referenced in the October 2020 General Conference talk, Love Your Enemies by President Dallin H. Oaks, First Presidency, annotation number 8.

For this past week, we finished the Book of Mormon, studying chapters 7-9.

Come Follow Me Preview: I Speak Unto You As If Ye Were Present

For this upcoming week, we will begin reading the Book of Ether, studying chapters 1-5.

The book of Ether is the record of the Jaredites, who arrived in the promised land centuries before the Nephites. God inspired Moroni to include Ether’s record in the Book of Mormon because of its relevance to our day. How do you feel it is relevant to your life?
